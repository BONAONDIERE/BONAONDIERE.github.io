About

From circuits to
dashboards

My path into data came through engineering — and that background changes how I think about every dataset I touch.

I started with a BSc in Electronics and Computer Engineering, designing electrical systems and building IoT infrastructure. When I joined a tractor-sharing platform and found myself processing sensor data from 500+ machines across East Africa, something shifted. The hardware was built. What the business actually needed was someone who could make sense of what it was reporting — in real time, without a 6-hour wait.

That experience shaped everything I do now. I've built PySpark ETL pipelines handling millions of records, designed Tableau and Power BI dashboards that teams check before their morning meetings, and taken ML models from a Jupyter notebook to something a risk team actually uses. Right now I'm a Data Analyst at Infotrace Analytics — writing ETL scripts, building sales and churn dashboards, and presenting weekly findings directly to management.

I'm also halfway through a Master's in AI and Technology Management at Nexford University — not because I needed more credentials, but because I kept hitting the same problem: organisations that have the models but don't know how to act on them. That's the gap I want to close.

I want a remote role where a dashboard I build on a Tuesday changes a decision made on Wednesday. If that's the kind of team you're building, I'd like to talk.
